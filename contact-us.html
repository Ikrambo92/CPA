    • Business Rates

      Business rates are a tax on non-domestic properties in the United Kingdom. They are calculated based on the rateable value of the property, which is a measure of its annual rental value. The rateable value is multiplied by a multiplier to determine the amount of business rates payable.

      The 2023 revaluation of business rates has resulted in an increase in the rateable value of many properties. This means that businesses will be paying more in business rates in 2023. The impact of this increase will vary depending on the size and location of the business.

      Small businesses are likely to be most affected by the increase in business rates. This is because they are more likely to be located in high-value areas, where the rateable value has increased significantly. The increase in business rates could put a strain on small businesses' finances and make it difficult for them to compete with larger businesses.

      The government has introduced a number of reliefs to help businesses with the increase in business rates. These reliefs include a 75% discount for retail, hospitality, and leisure businesses, and a 100% discount for businesses with a rateable value of less than £12,000. However, these reliefs will not be enough to offset the increase in business rates for all businesses.
